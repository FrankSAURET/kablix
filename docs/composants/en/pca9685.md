# 16-channel PWM driver (PCA9685)

![16-channel PWM driver (PCA9685)](../img/pca9685.png)

Seeed **16-Channel PWM Driver** Grove module (ref. 108020102), based on the
NXP **PCA9685**: 16 12-bit PWM outputs on an I²C bus, designed to drive
servo motors or LEDs. 16 servo connectors **P1 to P16** (channels 0 to 15).

## Pins

| Pin | Role |
|--------|------|
| **SDA / SCL** | I²C bus (Grove connector, left) |
| **VCC / GND** | Logic power of the chip (Grove connector) |
| **V+ / GND.2** | Servo power terminal (**Power In**, right) |
| **PWM0…PWM15** | Signal of each servo connector (P1 = PWM0 … P16 = PWM15) |
| **Pn.5V / Pn.GND** | Power of each servo connector (red/black) |

## Properties

| Property | Role | Default |
|-----------|------|--------|
| `ad0` … `ad5` | State of the board's six address pads (checked = pad **high**) | all checked |

## I²C address

![Address pads, under the board](../img/pca9685%20-%20dessous.png)

The address is not chosen from a list: it is set **like on the real
board**, by checking the six pads **AD0 to AD5** in the inspector (visible on the
back of the module, above). The resulting 7-bit address is shown under the boxes
and equals:

```
address = 1 A5 A4 A3 A2 A1 A0
```

Bit 6 is **wired high** on the module and bit 7 does not exist: the address
therefore ranges from **0x40** (all pads low) to **0x7F** (all high).

| AD5 | AD4 | AD3 | AD2 | AD1 | AD0 | Address |
|:---:|:---:|:---:|:---:|:---:|:---:|---------|
| 0 | 0 | 0 | 0 | 0 | 0 | `0x40` |
| 0 | 0 | 0 | 0 | 0 | 1 | `0x41` |
| 0 | 0 | 0 | 0 | 1 | 0 | `0x42` |
| … | … | … | … | … | … | … |
| 1 | 1 | 1 | 1 | 1 | 1 | `0x7F` |

> The Grove board ships from the factory with **all its pads high → 0x7F**
> (unlike Adafruit's bare PCA9685 module, whose pads are low → 0x40: uncheck the
> six boxes to simulate it). This is the address your program must
> use. On several boards on the same bus, give each a different pad
> combination.

## External power supply required

The servo motors are **not** powered by the board's logic: you must
connect a **[bench power supply](alim.md)** (*Instruments* category)
set to **~5 V** at a **sufficient current** on
the **V+ / GND.2** terminal (Power In).

- Without this supply (or outside the 4.5–5.5 V range, or with insufficient current),
  the outputs **do not move** — the chip still responds on I²C, exactly
  like the real board.
- Each servo draws ~0.2 A: adjust the supply's *maximum supplied current*.

## Usage

- Wire SDA/SCL/VCC/GND to the microcontroller, the V+/GND.2 terminal to the supply.
- Plug a servo (PWM/V+/GND) into a **P1…P16** connector.
- The PCA9685 generates 16 PWM signals at 50 Hz; a 0.5–2.5 ms pulse gives
  an angle of 0–180°.
- Address the PCA9685 registers (MODE1, PRESCALE, LED0_ON_L…) over I²C, or
  use a dedicated library (Adafruit PCA9685, `grove_16_channels_pwm`…).
